"For any integers m and n, if m and n are both even then their sum is even."

Assume m and n are even. If $m \in \mathbb{Z}$ and $n \in \mathbb{Z}$
, then since $m=2a$ and $n=2b$ would have the sum $m+n=2a+2b$, there 
would be an integer $k \in \mathbb{Z}$ that equals $2a + 2b$ which 
is divisible by 2. This makes the sum of m an n even.
